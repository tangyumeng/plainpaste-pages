PlainPaste Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 26, 2026

Summary

PlainPaste is a local macOS utility for pasting plain text. PlainPaste does not collect personal data, does not require an account, and does not upload clipboard contents.

Data collection

PlainPaste does not collect, sell, rent, share, or transmit personal data. The app does not include account registration, analytics, advertising tracking, or cloud synchronization.

Permissions

Accessibility permission is used only when you choose automatic paste. PlainPaste uses this permission locally to send the standard Command + V shortcut to the frontmost app after you trigger PlainPaste.

Clipboard contents

PlainPaste reads clipboard text only to convert it to plain text on your Mac. Clipboard content is not stored, uploaded, logged, or shared.

Changes to this policy

If PlainPaste changes how it handles privacy in a future release, this policy will be updated on this page.
